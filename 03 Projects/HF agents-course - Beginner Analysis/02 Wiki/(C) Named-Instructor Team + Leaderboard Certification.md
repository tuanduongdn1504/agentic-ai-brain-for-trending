## 5. Leaderboard certification model

### How it works

- **Unit 4:** Automated evaluation of student agents
- **Leaderboard:** Ranking of student results
- **Peer comparison:** Students see others' performance
- **No formal credential:** No certificate or transferable credit

### Benefits

1. **Gamification** — ranking drives engagement
2. **Portfolio evidence** — leaderboard placement on resume
3. **Peer learning** — top students can help others
4. **Cost efficiency** — no credentialing infrastructure needed

### Limitations

1. **No transferable credential** — students can't list as degree
2. **Zero-sum competition** — leaderboard may discourage below-average students
3. **Gaming risk** — students may optimize for leaderboard, not learning
4. **Evaluation dependency** — automated evaluation must be robust

## 9. Educational philosophy inferred

### Instructor-centric pedagogy

HF v26's model suggests:
1. **Individual expertise matters** — named credit = individual-driven quality
2. **Community connection via individuals** — students follow instructors
3. **Specialization per unit** — each instructor brings different expertise

### Microsoft v6 philosophy contrast

v6 Microsoft suggests:
1. **Institutional continuity** — content survives individual departures
2. **Consistent corporate voice** — Microsoft-branded experience
3. **Scale via anonymous contribution** — more contributors, less attribution

### Both valid

Different pedagogical choices serve different organizational goals.

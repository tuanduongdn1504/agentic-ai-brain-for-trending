## 6. 3 new Pattern candidates at v19

### #28 Multi-Provider AI Abstraction

**Formal statement:**
> "Agent-space frameworks increasingly adopt provider-abstraction layers (LiteLLM, OneAPI, OpenRouter, LangChain ChatModels) to decouple framework from specific AI vendor. Benefits: cost/quality tradeoff, vendor-switching, self-hosted support (Ollama), fallback resilience."

**Evidence at v19:** 1 (TrendRadar LiteLLM)

**Required for promotion:** 2+ more frameworks with multi-provider abstraction.

**Prediction:** Will validate — LangChain + LiteLLM widespread outside corpus.

### #29 GPL-3.0 in Agent-Space

**Formal statement:**
> "Copyleft licensing (GPL-3.0, AGPL) emerges as community-preservation commitment in agent-space, distinct from permissive-license (MIT, Apache-2.0) majority."

**Evidence at v19:** 1 (TrendRadar GPL-3.0)

**Prior corpus:**
- MIT: 14 wikis
- Apache-2.0: 1 wiki (gws)
- ISC: 1 wiki (codymaster)
- **GPL-3.0: 1 wiki (TrendRadar) — FIRST**

**Required for promotion:** 2+ more GPL frameworks.

**Prediction:** May remain rare — most corpus is MIT. Copyleft is ethic choice.

### #30 3-Layer Agent Ecosystem Stratification

**Formal statement:**
> "As agent ecosystem matures, 3 distinct standardization layers emerge:
> 1. **Transport (MCP)** — universal across platforms
> 2. **Provider abstraction (LiteLLM)** — emerging standard
> 3. **Runtime identifiers (OpenClaw/Hermes)** — community-platform + regional-specific
> 
> Frameworks adopt layers independently."

**Evidence at v19:** 1 framework fully exemplifies (TrendRadar adopts L1 + L2 + skips L3)

**Required for promotion:** 2+ frameworks with explicit layered adoption pattern.

**Prediction:** Strong pattern — likely promotes at v20+.

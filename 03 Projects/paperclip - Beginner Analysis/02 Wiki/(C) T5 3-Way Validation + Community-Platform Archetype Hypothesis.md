## 6. Community-platform archetype — hypothesis detail

### Defining characteristics (if valid)

**A community-platform archetype project:**
- No corporate parent disclosed (distinguishes from corporate-generalist)
- But polished + funded-appearing (distinguishes from solo-specialist)
- Open-source with MIT/Apache license
- Multi-contributor but no named individual maintainers
- Community infrastructure (Discord, plugin registry, forums)
- Active roadmap + regular releases
- Production-grade infrastructure (not hobby)
- Funding source either: self-funded team / crowdfunding / foundation / stealth-commercial

### Potential other examples (in wider ecosystem)

- **AutoGPT** — 160K+ stars, community-led post-pivot, opaque governance
- **BabyAGI** — viral start, community continuation
- **CrewAI** — transitioned from solo to company, ambiguous period
- **LangChain** — started community, now corporate (crossed archetype)

### If community-platform is real archetype

Predicts:
- Future T2/T3/T4 validations may also have third archetype
- Pattern #9 becomes richer: corporate / solo / community triple
- Storm Bear corpus value grows (nuanced archetype reading)

### If community-platform is illusion

Falls into:
- Stealth-corporate (truly funded but hidden)
- Transitional (between solo founding and corporate scaling)

## 8. Edges + failure modes (of analysis)

### Over-pattern-matching
- 14 wikis is not large sample; 2-observation patterns may be coincidence
- Corporate-vs-solo could be descriptive convenience not structural axis
- Adding categories ad-hoc risks unfalsifiable classification

### Under-pattern-matching
- Dismissing paperclip as edge-case when it's genuine signal
- Missing community-platform archetype presence in other wikis (retrospective reanalysis needed)

### Interpretation bias
- Analyst may prefer richer taxonomy (A) because it's more interesting
- Or prefer clean bifurcation (B) for parsimony
- Stealth-corporate (C) requires adversarial assumption rarely warranted

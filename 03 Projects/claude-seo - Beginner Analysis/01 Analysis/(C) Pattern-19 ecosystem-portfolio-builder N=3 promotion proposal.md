## Mechanism specifiability test (criterion #4)

**Proposed Pattern #19 ecosystem-portfolio-builder definition** (drafted for v66 audit):

> A founder/maintainer of an open-source artifact **publishes ≥2 functionally-coupled products under a shared brand or org** with **public ecosystem narrative** (README ecosystem section / portfolio-builder author claim / cross-product workflow). The portfolio strategy is **explicit, not coincidental**: products reference each other, share branding, or chain workflows.

**Test against future subjects:**
- ✅ A solo author with 1 skill + 1 unrelated tool under same GitHub org WITHOUT cross-product narrative → NOT qualifying (no portfolio narrative)
- ✅ A solo author with 1 skill + a fork of someone else's project → NOT qualifying (not author-original portfolio)
- ✅ A solo author with 2 functionally-coupled products + cross-promotion → QUALIFYING (matches definition)
- ✅ A corporate-team with 5 products + ecosystem section → CANNOT BE STRICT PORTFOLIO-BUILDER (not founder-personal; falls into different Pattern #19 archetype)

The mechanism is specifiable and **discriminates from cases that look superficially similar** (corporate ecosystem; coincidental same-author projects).

## Discrimination from existing Pattern #19 sub-types (criterion #5)

Pattern #19 already has confirmed archetypes:
- **founder-personal → org-stewardship**: 1 product transitions from founder to org over time (sister to v59 AutoGPT)
- **first-mover-authority-without-lineage** (NEW v59/v61 sub-type): solo author claims authority without external lineage; can be single-product

**ecosystem-portfolio-builder** is distinct because:
- It REQUIRES ≥2 products simultaneously (multi-product axis)
- It REQUIRES explicit ecosystem narrative (not just coincidental same-author projects)
- It is COMPATIBLE with first-mover-authority-without-lineage at multi-product scale (forrestchang and AgriciDaniel both claim authority without external lineage; gotalab inherits from Kiro IDE methodology)

The sub-type can co-occur with other Pattern #19 archetypes — but it's a distinct structural claim about portfolio scale + narrative coherence.

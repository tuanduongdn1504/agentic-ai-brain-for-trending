## 4. CN-primary as bilingual-adjacency for VN operator

### 4.1 VN-CN technical-vocabulary overlap

VN technical + academic vocabulary inherits significantly from CN via historical cultural exchange + shared East-Asian technical nomenclature:
- 模型 (model) ↔ VN "mô hình" (same Sino-Vietnamese etymology)
- 微调 (fine-tuning) ↔ VN often borrows Japanese-originating "fine-tuning" directly; CN-CJK-literate VN reader can parse character-meaning
- 提示 (prompt) ↔ CN literal; VN "gợi ý" or borrowed
- 大模型 (large model) ↔ VN "mô hình lớn" or "LLM"

**Estimated parse-ability for VN technical reader:** 60-80% of CN academic text in LLM-domain. Much higher than random-Chinese prose.

### 4.2 Mitigation strategies

**Option 1:** Use chrome translate extension for browsing GitHub PDFs / READMEs (acceptable for technical content; ~90%+ accuracy)

**Option 2:** Focus on Jupyter notebook code (code + English-ML-vocabulary dominates; Chinese comments are minority)

**Option 3:** Pair with Microsoft v6 or HF v26 EN courses for overlap topics; dive-into-llms fills CN-unique coverage (chapters 4 math reasoning; chapter 7 steganography — less available elsewhere)

### 4.3 Pedagogical-register considerations

CN academic pedagogy differs from Anglo-academic pedagogy:
- More formal titles / honorific attributions (老师 / 教授)
- More question-hook pedagogy (直接问读者; more engaged/conversational)
- Heavier metaphor + analogies (see chapter 6 "pry mouth of LLM", chapter 7 "invisible ink")
- Explicit "advanced exercises" + "extended reading" at chapter end (CN academic tradition of self-directed depth)

For VN operator, this register may feel more natural than Anglo-academic register due to cultural-adjacent academic traditions. Potential enjoyment factor.

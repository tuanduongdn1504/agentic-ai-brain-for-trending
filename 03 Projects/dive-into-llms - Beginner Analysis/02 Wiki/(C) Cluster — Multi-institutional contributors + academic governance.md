## 5. CN-academic-open-materials-tradition observation (corpus-first observational)

**Cultural/legal context:**
- Chinese academic materials (尤其是 course handouts + 讲义) have traditionally circulated via email / physical photocopying / departmental websites
- OSS-license adoption in CN academic settings grew slowly (compared to CN commercial OSS which is LLC-driven)
- Public-welfare (公益) framing implies liberal-use informally without explicit license grant
- Default-copyright under CN law similar to Western-Berne-Convention: copyright exists on creation; absence of license = all-rights-reserved but practical enforcement unlikely for academic-non-commercial reuse

**Implications for corpus:**
- Pattern #29 absent-LICENSE sub-category now N=2 (bizos v37 + dive-into-llms v39) — both have culturally-contextual defaults
- Storm Bear wiki consumers MUST treat dive-into-llms content as "read-only + learn-only" without permission for fork/redistribute/adapt
- Academic-institutional informal-reuse norms ≠ legal-OSS clarity
